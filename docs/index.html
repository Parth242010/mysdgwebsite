Scale of the Problem

Over 700 million people live in extreme poverty, surviving on less than $2.15 a day. Without urgent action, millions will remain in poverty by 2030.

Root Causes

Unemployment, low wages, poor education, limited healthcare, corruption, natural disasters, and wars ‚Äî all reinforce the cycle of poverty.

Consequences

Poverty leads to malnutrition, illiteracy, poor health, inequality, crime, and instability. Children in poverty are more likely to drop out of school, work in unsafe conditions, or marry early.
